📖 Study Guide — how to crack GATE 2027

How to use this tracker

  • Every subject holds the full GATE syllabus as topics — tick each topic as you finish it.
  • Add your own topics (sub-topics, doubts, revision items) with the box under each subject.
  • Drag the ⠿ handle to reorder topics or subjects — the order is saved.
  • Progress bars (top + per subject) update automatically.

7 ground rules

  1. Quality > hours. 8 focused hours beat 12 distracted ones.
  2. PYQs are king. Solve 10–15 years of previous-year questions.
  3. Mocks build rank. Full timed mocks + deep analysis from Month 5.
  4. Spiral revision. Re-touch old subjects via daily PYQs.
  5. One rest day/week. Protect your sleep (7 hrs).
  6. Make short notes for every subject — your last-week revision.
  7. Don't fail college — but the syllabus overlaps, so it's synergistic.

Priority tiers (your dev background)

  • 🟢 Quick: Programming & DS, DBMS, General Aptitude — blitz these.
  • 🟡 Moderate: OS, Networks, Algorithms, Digital Logic, Maths — drill problems.
  • 🔴 Heavy (start early): Theory of Computation, Discrete Math, COA, Compiler.

Daily routine (10 hours)

  • ~4h — new concepts (1 heavy + 1 lighter subject)
  • ~2h — problem-solving on today's topics
  • ~1.5h — PYQs of already-covered subjects (spiral revision)
  • ~1.5h — Aptitude + Engineering Maths (daily habit)
  • ~1h — revise the day + update short notes

How to study one topic (the flow)

  1. Confirm it's in the syllabus (it's in this tracker).
  2. Check its PYQ frequency (how important) on GATE Overflow.
  3. Understand it — video for heavy subjects, book for your strong ones — once.
  4. Make short notes.
  5. Solve problems yourself (this is 70% of learning).
  6. Stuck? Ask AI or GATE Overflow.
  7. Solve that topic's PYQs — this confirms you've studied enough.
  8. Revise after 1 day / 1 week (active recall).

Is a topic really asked in GATE? (verify)

  • Syllabus = boundary. Not in the syllabus → don't study it.
  • PYQs = reality. On gateoverflow.in, filter a topic → see how many questions came in 10–15 years. Many → invest more; zero → skip depth.
  • Check a weightage chart (marks per topic) and give time proportional to marks, not to how hard/interesting it is.
  • Match depth to PYQs — study only as deep as GATE actually asks.
  • If a finished topic has no PYQs at all, you over-invested — move on faster next time.

How to remember (retention)

  • Active recall — close the book and test yourself; don't re-read passively.
  • Spaced repetition — revisit after 1 day → 1 week → 1 month.
  • Solve, don't just read — retrieval builds memory.
  • Short notes / formula sheets — reuse for revision.
  • Feynman — explain it simply (to AI or aloud); gaps show up instantly.

PYQ & mock strategy

  • PYQ source: GATE Overflow (free) + Made Easy / ACE books. Solve subject-wise, then full papers. Keep a mistake log.
  • From Month 5: 3–4 full mocks/week, strictly timed. The 2–3h analysis matters more than the mock. Track your score trend.

Resources (pick one per subject)

  • PYQs + doubts: GATE Overflow · NPTEL for deep lectures
  • Videos: Neso Academy (TOC, DL, CN) · Gate Smashers (OS, DBMS, CN, COA, Compiler) · Abdul Bari (Algorithms, DS) · Knowledge Gate (Discrete)
  • Test series: Made Easy / ACE / PW GATE Wallah / Unacademy · Aptitude: R.S. Aggarwal

Do's & Don'ts

  • ✅ Stick to the routine · make & reuse notes · solve PYQs from day one · rest weekly · focus on high-weightage topics.
  • ❌ Don't hoard 10 resources · don't skip aptitude/maths (28 easy marks) · don't do theory-only · don't ignore college · don't compare speed with others.
